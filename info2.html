Health & Wellness

Health care refers to the organized provision of medical care to individuals or communities. It encompasses a wide range of services provided to promote, maintain, monitor, and restore health.

Types of Health Care Services

  • Primary Care: The first point of contact for general health concerns (e.g., family doctors).
  • Specialized Care: Services provided by specialists like cardiologists, dermatologists, etc.
  • Emergency Care: Immediate care for urgent conditions (e.g., ER visits).
  • Preventive Care: Screenings, immunizations, and wellness visits to prevent illness.
  • Mental Health Services: Counseling, therapy, and psychiatric care.

Wellness Tips

  • Eat a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and whole grains.
  • Exercise regularly — at least 30 minutes a day, 5 days a week.
  • Get adequate sleep (7–8 hours per night).
  • Manage stress through meditation, hobbies, or social interaction.
  • Stay hydrated and avoid excessive consumption of alcohol or processed foods.

Did You Know?

Preventive care can help detect diseases early, often before symptoms occur — increasing chances of successful treatment and reducing overall healthcare costs.
